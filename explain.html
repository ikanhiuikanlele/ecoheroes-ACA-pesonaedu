All About Climate Change

About this Website

We made this website because we want more kids and teenagers to understand that helping the Earth can be simple, fun, and something anyone can do. Climate change is a serious problem, but many young people feel confused or think their actions are too small to matter. Through this website, we want to show that even small steps, like saving electricity, reducing trash, or learning about the environment, can create a big impact when many people do them together. By exploring missions, growing a virtual seed, reading short articles, and designing a clean version of the Earth, users can learn in a fun and interactive way. Our goal is to inspire young people to start taking small actions every day and realize that protecting the Earth starts with them.

What is Climate Change?

Climate change is the long-term shift in Earth's average weather patterns, primarily driven by human activities like burning fossil fuels, leading to a rise in global temperatures (global warming), more extreme weather, melting ice, and rising sea levels, disrupting natural systems. While natural climate shifts occur, the rapid warming since the 1800s is overwhelmingly caused by human-released greenhouse gases (like CO2 and methane) trapping heat, creating a warming blanket around the planet.

Cause and Effect of Climate Change

Causes of Climate Change

Climate change happens because of many actions done by humans. One of the biggest causes is the release of too much carbon dioxide and other gases into the atmosphere. These gases come from things like cars, factories, burning coal and oil, and cutting down forests. When trees are removed, there are fewer plants to absorb carbon dioxide, which makes the problem even worse.

Using a lot of electricity, wasting energy, and producing large amounts of trash also add to the problem. All these activities make the Earth trap more heat, which causes the temperature of the planet to rise.

Effect of Climate Change

Climate change affects the Earth in many ways. The temperature of the planet becomes hotter, which can lead to stronger heat waves. Weather becomes more extreme, including heavier storms, floods, and droughts. Ice in the polar regions melts faster, and this causes sea levels to rise.

Animals and plants also suffer because their homes change too quickly. Some species may struggle to survive. Climate change can also affect people. It can damage homes, reduce clean water, harm crops, and make some places unsafe to live in.

How to Solve This Problem?

We can help solve climate change by taking small actions every day. One way is to use energy wisely, such as turning off lights when we are not using them and unplugging chargers. We can also reduce waste by reusing items, recycling, and choosing products that are better for the environment.

Planting trees and protecting nature are important because trees absorb carbon dioxide and help clean the air. Using cleaner transportation, like walking, biking, or taking public transport, also helps reduce pollution.

Saving water, learning more about the environment, and sharing what we know with others can inspire more people to join in. Even though climate change is a big problem, small steps done by many people can create a big positive impact for our planet.

Current Data

Increase in CO₂ concentration since pre-industrial times : ~50% The amount of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere has risen about 50 percent compared with levels in the late 1700s, mainly due to human activities like burning fossil fuels.

Increase in greenhouse gas warming effect since 1990 : 51% The total warming influence of all human-emitted greenhouse gases in the atmosphere increased by 51% compared to 1990 levels.

Methane atmospheric increase : more than 100% since pre-industrial era Methane levels have more than doubled since pre-industrial times, driven by agriculture and fossil fuel use.

Largest annual CO₂ rise on record : +3.75 ppm in 2024 In 2024, atmospheric carbon dioxide concentration rose by 3.75 parts per million, the largest one-year increase measured.

Global average temperature above pre-industrial levels : ~1.5°C or higher Recent years have seen global temperatures about 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels.

Probability of exceeding 1.5°C in coming years : ~80% There is about an 80% chance that the global average temperature will exceed 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels.

Increase in wildfire CO₂ emissions : +9% in 2024 Global carbon emissions from wildfires increased by 9% in 2024.

Increase in climate-related disasters in Indonesia (2010-2022) : +82% The number of climate-related disasters in Indonesia rose by 82% between 2010 and 2022.
